How to Use an Epipen

Step One:

Remove the EpiPen from its carrier tube

Step Two:

Take off the blue safety cap from the top of the EpiPen

Step Three:

Hold the EpiPen in your dominant hand with the orange tip pointing downwards

Step Four:

Place the orange tip against the outer thigh. (It can be administered through clothing if necessary)

Step Five:

Push the EpiPen firmly into the thigh until you hear a click, hold it in place for for three seconds

Step Six:

After three seconds, remove the EpiPen from the thigh and massage the injection site for ten seconds

Step Seven:

Call 911 immediately after using the EpiPen, even if symptoms appear to improve as the symptoms of the EpiPen can wear off after 20 minutes

Step Eight:

Place the EpiPen back into its carrier tube and give it to the emergency responders for proper disposal

When to Use an Epipen

An Epi-Pen should be used when experiencing severe anaphylaxis. Signs to look out for are:

  • Difficulty Breathing: Wheezing, shortness of breath, or tightness in the chest
  • Swelling: Swelling of the face, lips, throat, or tongue
  • Hives: Red, itchy hives that spread across the body
  • Gastrointestinal Symptoms: Nausea, diarrhea, vomiting, or stomach cramps
  • Dizziness: Feeling lightheaded or fainting
  • Rapid or Weak Pulse: Unusually fast or weak heartbeat

If any of these symptoms occur, administer the epinephrine and call emergency services.
